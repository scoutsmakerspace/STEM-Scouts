## Requirements

- **1.** Find out what happens when you add salt to water.
- **2.** Show how vinegar reacts with different items and explain what happens. The different items could be steel wool, sodium bicarbonate or old dirty coins.
- **3.** Compare the density of water to the density of other liquids. Show how these liquids react to each other.
- **4.** Make a pH indicator solution. Use it to test the acidity or alkalinity of other liquids.
- **5.** Grow crystals.
- **6.** Another activity agreed with your leader
- **1.** Find a way to show that hot air rises.
- **2.** Create a basic electrical circuit that includes a switch. Show how it could be used to control a lightbulb powered by a battery.
- **3.** Make an air-powered balloon rocket or a water rocket. Investigate ways of improving how far it can travel.
- **4.** Make a simple compass. Show the effects of metallic and magnetic materials upon it.
- **5.** Use marshmallows and spaghetti to build the strongest tower you can. Explain how you improved your design.
- **6.** Another activity agreed with your leader.
- **1.** Set up a wormery or ant colony. Record what happens over three or four weeks.
- **2.** Investigate what happens to your pulse rate before, during and after exercise.
- **3.** Grow cress, beans, peas or a similar plant. Investigate what happens when the plant has no access to light. What happens when it has light, but no water?
- **4.** Find a way to show that plants take water up through their stems.
- **5.** Make some yoghurt and find out how living organisms are involved in the process.
