## Requirements

- **1.** Using simple diagrams, illustrate latitude and longitude.
- **2.** Using a compass, show how an aircraft can be turned on to various compass headings.
- **3.** Show that you understand how a compass works, including the difference between magnetic, true and grid north.
- **4.** Explain what magnetic deviation is and how it applies to air navigation.
- **5.** Show that you know about the latest developments in electronic navigation aids.
- **6.** Headings and corresponding tracks.
- **7.** Topographical air map
- **8.** Draw the track between any two places at least 100 nautical miles apart.
- **9.** Show that you can calculate overhead flight times
- **10.** Gain a good working knowledge of charts, chart datum and symbols used.
- **11.** Complete two positioning tasks
- **12.** Show your skills in compass work by completing three tasks
- **13.** Learn how to use tide tables and tidal stream atlases.
- **14.** Learn how to use the marine log to obtain distance run and speed.
- **15.** Show you understand the buoyage system for United Kingdom coastal waters and other methods of marking dangers and channels.
- **16.** Show that you’re aware of the latest developments in electronic technology, like the Global Positioning System and electronic charts.
- **17.** Go on a coastal voyage of between four and six hours and act as the navigator.
