## Requirements

- **1.** Radio Amateur Licence (Foundation, Intermediate or Full)
- **2.** Learn the regulations governing the use of amateur radio equipment.
- **3.** Visit an amateur radio station.
- **4.** Log 25 different amateur radio stations. Note the date, time, call sign, frequency, readability and location. You may include some broadcast stations.
- **5.** Show how to tune a simple communications receiver.
- **6.** Give an example of a typical greetings message.
- **7.** Explain in simple terms how radio waves travel around the world.
- **8.** Learn the more commonly used HF and VHF amateur frequency bands.
- **9.** Learn the phonetic alphabet and define at least eight international Q code signals.
- **10.** Show that you can recognise call signs from the UK and near continent.
- **11.** Send and receive a short message by Morse code or semaphore at a rate of five words per minute.
- **12.** Show that you know the proper procedure for sending and receiving a message.
- **13.** Learn the International Phonetic Alphabet and define at least eight international Q code signals.
- **14.** Construct a simple Morse code oscillator and send a short message.
- **15.** Show you know how to use your mobile safely and how to keep it safe.
- **16.** Learn the meaning of these terms: SMS, MMS, 3G, 4G, WAP and Bluetooth.
- **17.** Send a creative text, multimedia or video message to invite a friend to a Scouts event.
- **18.** Manage a mobile phone address book and set up groups of contacts.
- **19.** Show you can text accurately at a rate of 50 characters per minute.
- **20.** Show you know the meaning of some popular chat abbreviations.
- **21.** Share photos and videos of a Scouting activity you’ve been involved with, using available technology.
- **22.** Manage an email address book and set up groups of contacts.
- **23.** Carry on a simple conversation for about 10 minutes.
- **24.** Act as an interpreter for a visitor who does not speak your native language.
- **25.** Write a letter of around 150 words.
- **26.** After a few minutes of study, translate a paragraph of basic text.
- **27.** Communicate with a person who does not speak your native language.
- **28.** Carry out a simple conversation for about 10 minutes.
- **29.** Use sign language to describe a Scouting experience to another person.
- **30.** Act as a translator for a short conversation between a sign language user and someone with no sign language experience.
- **31.** Invite a sign language user to talk to your Troop about what it’s like to have impaired hearing or speech.
- **32.** Help by translating for them during their visit.
