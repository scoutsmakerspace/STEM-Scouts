## Requirements

- **1.** Use a bicycle that is properly equipped. Keep it in good working order for at least six months.
- **2.** Show that you can carry out essential maintenance and repairs
- **3.** Bikeability Level 2 or 3
- **4.** Explain what extra precautions you should take when cycling in the dark or in wet weather. Show you understand why motor vehicles take longer to stop in the wet.
- **5.** Learn the basics of first aid and what to do if an accident happens.
- **6.** Develop a working knowledge of map reading. Orientate a map using a compass or conspicuous features. Estimate distances and times taken to travel.
- **7.** Plan and carry out an all-day cycle ride of at least 40 kilometres (25 miles).
- **8.** Complete one of these: a. Show you can control a cycle along a slalom course b.Show you understand the Highway Code, including road signs and helmet use
- **9.** Show you understand the Mountain Bike Code of Conduct.
- **10.** Show you can control your cycle over different types of terrain.
- **11.** Show you're aware of the damage that may be caused to the environment through careless cycling across the countryside
- **12.** Learn the basics of first aid, including the treatment of hypothermia and find out what to do in the case of an accident.
- **13.** Gain a working knowledge of map reading. Orientate a map using a compass or conspicuous features. Estimate distances and times taken to travel
- **14.** Plan and carry out an all-day ride of at least 30 kilometres (20 miles)
- **15.** The Gold Trix Award of the British Schools Cycling Association
- **16.** Level 3 Go-MTB Award from Cycling Scotland, Sustrans, CTC or Scottish Cycling MBLA
