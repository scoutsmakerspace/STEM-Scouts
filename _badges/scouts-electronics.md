## Requirements

- Complete ALL of the following:
- 1. 1. Identify a number of common electronic components that are shown to you.
- 2. 2. Describe the systems used for marking components with their values.
- 3. 3. Show that you know the symbols used to represent common components in circuit diagrams.
- 4. 4. Show that you’re aware of the safe working practices to be followed when handling electronic components, circuit boards and when soldering.
- 5. 5. Use a multimeter to measure voltage, current and resistance in a simple circuit.
- 6. 6. Talk about the main differences in the operation of digital and analogue circuits.
- 7. 7. Construct three simple circuits.
- 8. Show how to identify the polarity of a diode and a specific PIN on an integrated circuit.
- 9. 8. Explain the principles behind the operation of each circuit and the typical values of voltage and current found in each.
- 10. 9. Explain the possible uses of the three circuits you built.
- 11. 10. Talk about the uses and benefits of electronics in everyday life.
