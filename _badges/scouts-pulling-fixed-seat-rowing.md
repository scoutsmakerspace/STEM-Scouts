## Requirements

- **1.** Point out and name the basic parts of a pulling boat
- **2.** Explain the importance of wearing appropriate clothing for various weather conditions
- **1.** Understand how to safely enter and exit the boat
- **2.** Understand the safety equipment required and how to use it
- **3.** Gain a basic knowledge of the rules of the road for your local waters
- **4.** Understand the difference between a lifejacket and a buoyancy aid, and demonstrate how to wear them correctly
- **5.** From a boat, heave an unweighted line to land within reach of target five metres, within three attempts
- **6.** Learn the actions and safety requirements to take when being towed
- **7.** Know how to respond in an emergency
- **1.** Take the boat away from a bank side mooring
- **2.** Row in a straight line for 100 metres
- **3.** Complete a figure-of-eight course
- **4.** Come alongside in your dinghy, to moor at a ring, post, bollard or buoy, using a round turn and two half hitches
- **1.** Cull over the stern between two points, 20 metres apart, and turn through 180 degrees
- **2.** Draw stroke over the bow
- **3.** Take a place as an oarsman, including stroke
- **4.** Give boat orders effectively, or listen to boat orders and act on them
- **5.** As bowman, be a lookout and report hazards to the coxswain using standard maritime directions, such as starboard, port quarter and dead ahead
