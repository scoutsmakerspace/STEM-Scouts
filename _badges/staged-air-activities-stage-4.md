## Requirements

- **1.** Trim a paper aeroplane or model glider to glide, stall and turn, then find out how an aircraft’s trimming, weight and balance can affect its flight.
- **2.** Draw a runway and the circuit pattern that planes follow, then explain how air traffic control uses this circuit to keep planes safe and communicate with pilots.
- **3.** Learn how the wind’s speed and direction are measured, then find out why they’re important during take-off and landing.
- **1.** Try an air-based activity and share what you learned.
- **2.** Find out about the roles and responsibilities of flight crew members, such as pilots, co-pilots, engineers or cabin crew.
- **3.** Simulate a take-off and landing using a flight simulator.
- **4.** Explain why pilot's (and other flight crew) use a pre-flight checklist.
- **5.** Explore the different types of emergency radio calls used in aviation, such as mayday and pan-pan
- **6.** Follow the flight path of an aircraft using maps and satellite images and see how landmarks help pilots find their way.
- **7.** Learn why Morse code is still used by navigational beacons and recognise six three-letter sequences.
