## Requirements

- **1.** Find out about the different types of air traffic control services used at airfields and airports.
- **2.** Explain why aircrafts have flaps, slots and slats and find out how each of them works.
- **3.** Design and build a model of a part of an aircraft and explain how it helps the plane fly.
- **4.** Explore the relationship between aviation and climate change.
- **5.** Explore a new aviation technology or come up with your own idea for a futuristic invention.
- **6.** Learn about the emergency procedures for one specific type of aircraft.
- **7.** Find a major flight path that’s close to you and explain how it’s chosen and why it helps make air travel safe and efficient.
- **8.** Take part in an air activity you haven't tried before or show a reasonable level of skill in an air activity you already practise.
- **9.** Learn what runway markings mean, then explain how lights and pyrotechnic signals are used for ground-to-aircraft communication.
- **10.** Learn about the different types of airspace, such as controlled, uncontrolled and special use.
- **11.** Work with a team and take on different roles (such as pilot, co-pilot, air traffic control, air marshal, and ground crew) to simulate safely completing a flight.
