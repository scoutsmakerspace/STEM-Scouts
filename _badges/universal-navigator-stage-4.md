## Requirements

- Complete ALL of the following:
- 1. Show you know how to: Convert grid bearings to magnetic bearings and vice versa. Use back bearings to check the route. Estimate your current position using a compass. Walk on a bearing. This should include ‘deviating from course’ (the four right angles technique to bypass an obstacle also known as 'boxing'). Read a six figure grid reference.
- 2. Using 1:50000 and 1:25000 scale Ordnance Survey maps: Interpret contour lines in terms of shape and steepness of terrain. Learn what the topographical features mean, including: Valley, Ridge, Spur, Col. Show how to set a map, with and without a compass. Learn how to use and give six-figure grid references. Demonstrate the use of a roamer to improve accuracy.
- 3. Walk two compass routes of at least 5 kilometres each.
- 4. Show you know how to dress appropriately for the walk and what kit you and your group need.
- 5. Choose the most appropriate type of map for the journey you are taking.
